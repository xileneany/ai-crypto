Top 5 AI stories
1

OpenAI launches GPT-Rosalind for drug discovery

OpenAI's first purpose-built domain model, focused on biochemistry, genomics, and translational medicine. Available in research preview to Amgen and Moderna. Drug discovery AI investment hit $2.51B in 2026; total since 2019 is $17B with zero AI-developed drugs yet in large-scale trials. The real test: citation rate in peer-reviewed papers, not benchmarks.

2

Snap cuts 1,000 jobs (16%), directly citing AI

CEO Evan Spiegel cited "rapid advancements in AI" enabling smaller teams. AI already generates 65%+ of Snap's new code. Expected to cut $500M+ in annualized costs by H2 2026. Stock jumped 7% on the news. This is the clearest corporate admission yet that AI is replacing headcount at scale — not augmenting it.

3

Anthropic's Mythos triggers Fed bank summit + White House access

The Mythos model's capabilities triggered a Fed-led meeting of bank CEOs over systemic risk implications. Separately, reports emerged the White House is moving to grant US agencies access to Mythos. Anthropic also launched Claude Opus 4.7 as a "safer" alternative. This is the first time a model release has prompted a regulatory convening.

4

Stanford 2026 AI Index: insiders vs public = "a canyon"

400+ page report reveals AI capabilities are accelerating dramatically on benchmarks, but public perception and trust are diverging sharply from what insiders see. Both OpenAI and Anthropic flagged as heading toward IPOs this year. Local US governments increasingly blocking data center construction over energy concerns.

5

OpenAI's advertising pivot: $100B revenue target by 2030

Early ad pilot generated $100M annualized in two months. Full projection: $2.5B in 2026 ad revenue, $100B by 2030. The bet is that conversational intent signals are more valuable than search. The risk: it poisons trust in AI assistants at the exact moment trust is their core moat.

Top 5 crypto stories
1

Deutsche Börse buys $200M stake in Kraken at $13.3B valuation

Frankfurt Stock Exchange operator takes 1.5% stake covering trading, custody, settlement, and tokenized assets. Kraken also filed a confidential IPO application with the SEC the same day. Key signal: valuation down from $20B in Nov 2025 — real price discovery, not hype. This is TradFi formally acquiring crypto infrastructure, not just experimenting with it.

2

CLARITY Act: White House signals deal is "holding" — barely

White House crypto adviser Witt told CoinDesk TV the stablecoin yield compromise appears durable, but banks pushed back immediately. Senate returns from recess with a hard deadline: committee markup must finish by end of April to hit the July floor vote window before August recess. Miss that, and it slides past the 2026 midterms.

3

Bitcoin holds above $73K, whale wallets accumulating

For only the second time in 2026, wallets holding 10,000+ BTC recorded net inflows. BlackRock's IBIT pulled $284M in single-day inflows on Apr 17. BTC now shows 84% correlation with S&P 500 and 87% with gold — it's a macro asset now, whether crypto natives like it or not.

4

Japan reclassifies crypto as financial instrument

Japan's cabinet approved a bill treating digital assets as investment products, bringing insider trading bans, mandatory disclosures, and paving the way for bank holdings. A quiet but enormous move — one of the world's largest financial markets formally integrating crypto into its regulated framework, expected live by 2027.

5

Anthropic's MCP crosses 97M installs — becomes crypto-adjacent infrastructure

Every major AI provider now ships MCP-compatible tooling. The Linux Foundation announced it will take MCP under open governance. Increasingly, crypto protocols are building MCP connectors, making it the de facto agent-to-blockchain interface layer. This is the quiet infrastructure story of 2026.

Key emerging trend

Infrastructure convergence: AI agents are becoming crypto's killer app

The MCP protocol hitting 97M installs while crypto protocols rush to build connectors isn't coincidental. AI agents need verifiable, programmable money rails — and crypto needs a reason for autonomous agents to touch it. Anthropic Mythos triggering a Fed bank summit signals that regulators are starting to think about AI + finance as a single risk category, not two separate ones. Deutsche Börse buying into Kraken is the same thesis from the TradFi side: financial infrastructure is merging, and compliance is becoming a competitive moat. The companies winning are the ones building at the intersection.
